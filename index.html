Art Portfolio:
Nicole Birova

Some of my art...

Under the Sea

This painting was done in 2019, in watercolor. Techniques like wet-on-wet, lifting, and dry brush come together to create a hyper-realistic look at a sea turtle.

On the Edge

This painting, completed in 2020, is done in oil paint. Many techniques from oil were used, including griseille, wet-on-wet oil, color blocking, and more. It won numerous awards including the Congressional award, and the Scholastic Silver.

A Blurred Statue

This painting, completed in 2022, is done in oil paint. This painting is originally a famous sculpture of Laocoon, a figure in Greek and Roman mythology, and is a tribute to Richter's blurry pieces at the Art Institute of Chicago. It employs griseille and wet-on-wet for the final blur.

Two Selves

This piece, completed in 2022, is done in graphite and charcoal. It consists of two self-portraits, a lower, realistic piece, and an upper, distorted one. They are both produced with a mix of graphite and charcoal and represent different aspects of one person.

My name is Nicole Birova. I am a junior at Northwestern University studying Data Science, with minors in Computer Science and Fine Arts Theory and Practice. I have been drawing and painting since I was ten years old, so for about 11 years now. I have been professionally-trained in oil painting, but I complete works in many mediums including: graphite, charcoal, watercolor, and acrylic. skills

1

Watercolor

Artists' paint made with a water-soluble binder such as gum arabic, and thinned with water rather than oil, giving a transparent color.

2

Oil Paint

Oil paint is form of a slow-drying paint that consists of particles of pigment suspended in a drying oil that forms a tough, coloured film on exposure to air.

3

Acrylic Paint

Acrylic paint is a fast-drying paint made of pigment suspended in acrylic polymer emulsion and plasticizers.

4

Graphite/Charcoal

A lightweight black carbon residue produced by strongly heating wood in minimal oxygen to remove all water and volatile constituents.

5

Architectural Ink

Ink is a gel, sol, or solution that contains at least one colorant, such as a dye or pigment, and is used to color a surface.
